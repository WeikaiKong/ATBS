1University of Nottingham Ningbo China, China, 2Nottingham Ningbo China Beacons of Excellence Research and Innovation Institute,

Abstract

Cross-modal learning of video and text plays a key role in Video Question Answering (VideoQA), while insufficient language supervision in domain knowledge often challenges VideoQA models. In this paper, we propose a visual-text attention mechanism to utilize the Contrastive Language- Image Pre-training (CLIP) to guide the cross-modal learning for VideoQA tasks. Specifically, the proposed method en- codes video features using a TimeSformer and text features via a BERT-based model, and another pair of vision-language features with the support of general domain knowledge from CLIP. Then, the attention information across visual and lin- guistic features is extracted through a set of CLIP-guided visual-text encoders and integrated to predict the answer. The proposed method is evaluated on MSVD-QA and MSRVTT- QA datasets. It outperforms state-of-the-art methods.
